Other Interests and Experience!

My Experience at the Art League of Houston

The Art League of Houston (ALH) is a non-profit organization that provides art education and programming to people of all ages in the Houston community. In the summer of 2020, I had the incredible opportunity to participate in their Teen Summer Intensive Program as a student. The program offered an immersive experience in a variety of visual art disciplines led by professional artists and educators. Throughout the six-week program, we had the opportunity to collaborate with local Houston artists, while also working on a mural project, where we collaborated with our peers to create a large-scale artwork that was displayed publicly in the Houston community. At the end of the program, we participated in a community exhibition where we showcased the artwork we created during the program to fundraise for the ALH Healing Arts Program, geared to people with disabilities. The experience was so transformative that I was thrilled to be invited back in the summer of 2021 as an Alum Mentor for the Teen Summer Intensive Program. In this role, I was able to work with the new students and share my experiences and knowledge with them. Being a mentor allowed me to reflect on my own journey as an artist and how much I had learned from the program, providing me with invaluable experiences that have influenced my artistic growth and development.

My Knowledge and Passion for Coffee
